Irene's Wedding Nepali Momos

This is a short recipe provided to a collective cookbook given to a dear friend from all the guests of the wedding. It is a delicious recipe for Nepali style veg momos which are a popular snack or quick meal in Nepal.

I added this recipe because I had lived in Nepal before and wanted to share something from my life to add to her list. We had also made momos together when Irene and Georges were living in Thailand together.

Ingredients for Dough

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 3/4 cup water (adjust as needed)

Ingredients for Filling

  • 2 cups finely chopped cabbage
  • 1 cup grated carrot
  • 1 cup finely chopped onion
  • 1 cup finely chopped bell pepper (any color)
  • 1/2 cup crumbled tofu or paneer
  • 2 tablespoons momo masala
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • 1 tablespoon minced ginger
  • 2 tablespoons chopped cilantro
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil

Steps

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt.
  2. Add the vegetable oil and mix well.
  3. Gradually add water, mixing the dough until it forms a smooth, slightly elastic ball. You may need to adjust the water quantity depending on the flour type and humidity.
  4. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  5. In a large mixing bowl, combine all the filling ingredients and mix well.
  6. Roll out the rested momo dough into small, thin circles, about 3 inches in diameter.
  7. Place a spoonful of filling in the center of each dough circle.
  8. Fold and seal the edges of the dough to form a momo (half-moon or round shape).
  9. Steam the momos for 15-20 minutes in a steamer or a bamboo steamer until the dough is cooked through.
